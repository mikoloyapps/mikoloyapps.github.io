A crown of steel he bore, but between rim and robe naught was there to see, save only a deadly gleam of eyes: the Lord of the Nazgûl... now he was come again, bringing ruin, turning hope to despair, and victory to death. <53>The **Witch-king of Angmar**, also known as the **Lord of the Nazgûl**, was the leader of the Nazgûl (Ringwraiths) and Sauron's second-in-command during the Second and Third Ages of Middle-earth.

![](witchKing/2.jpg)

#### Witch-king and his Nazgûl

<53>His precise identity is unknown, but he could be one of three noble lords of Númenor, who were corrupted by the Nine Rings of Power and thus became the undying Wraiths in the service of Sauron. After Sauron's defeat by the Last Alliance, the Witch-king eventually reappeared in the 14th century of the Third Age to found the evil realm of Angmar, where he gained his infamous epithet and ruled for almost seven centuries until the three successor states of Arnor were finally conquered.

<53>The Witch-king returned to Mordor to facilitate Sauron's return to power, then took Gondor's city of Minas Ithil and refortified it as Minas Morgul, and snuffed out the line of Kings of Gondor. He led Sauron's armies in the War of the Ring, stabbed Frodo Baggins on Weathertop during the first months of Frodo's venture out of the Shire to Rivendell, and at the Battle of the Pelennor Fields he broke the Great Gate of Minas Tirith, killed King Théoden of Rohan, and met his own doom.

# History
---

## **The Second Age: the Nine Rings and the advent of the Nazgûl**

![](witchKing/3.jpg)

#### The Witch-king is gifted his Ring of Power as a human

<53>The forging of the Rings of Power began some 1500 years into the Second Age. In SA 1600, Sauron created and first put on the One Ring, and in SA 1693 the War of the Elves and Sauron began, during which the Dark Lord recovered the Nine Rings and gave them to powerful lords and rulers of Men. The most powerful of all of them was likely a king and a powerful sorcerer, possibly of Númenórean heritage, but little is known of his (or the others') earthly life save that over the coming centuries they prospered as their power, wealth, and mortal lifespans extended under the corrupting influence of their rings. The only certainty is that eventually he and all the others slipped into the Wraith-world and became one of the Nazgûl, or Ringwraiths, who were entirely enslaved to Sauron's will.

<53>The first sighting of the Nazgûl in Middle-earth was reported in SA 2251. For the next 1200 years the greatest among them, known simply as the Lord of the Nazgûl, would serve Sauron as commander of his army. He fought in the climactic war of the Age against the Last Alliance of Elves and Men between SA 3434 and SA 3441, when the Dark Lord was finally defeated and the One Ring was taken from him. With Sauron's spirit and power dissipated, the nine Nazgûl were no longer able to maintain their physical presence and they too disappeared - at least temporarily - from Middle-earth.

## **The Third Age: return and the rise of Angmar**

![](witchKing/4.jpg)

#### The Witch-king's true undead appearance in the first Lord of the Rings movie

<53>A thousand years into the Third Age, Sauron took shape in the world again and, incognito, founded the fortress of Dol Guldur in southern Mirkwood in TA 1050. This signaled the return of the Nine to Middle-earth. Their chief, having regained power, would set about executing his master's long vengeance upon the Realms in Exile of the Dúnedain, the descendants of the Faithful of Númenor who had escaped his destruction and who subsequently brought him down with the Last Alliance.

<53>By this time the northern Dúnedain realm of Arnor, sister-kingdom to southern Gondor, had already fractured into three warring successor states - Rhudaur, Cardolan and Arthedain. It was into this feverish situation that the Lord of the Nazgûl reappeared in TA 1300 and began to fulfill Sauron's plan to bring down the North Kingdom entirely. Adjacent to its territories he founded the dark domain of Angmar - where, from its capital of Carn Dûm, he began his campaign of open war against the Dúnedain of Arnor. It was during this series of conflicts that they labeled the undead sorcerer-chieftain of their new enemy with the title "Witch-king, Lord of Angmar".

## **The Witch-king's conquest of the North**

![](witchKing/5.jpg)

#### The Witch-king on his throne of Angmar

<53>In TA 1356, the "Witch-king" began his assault against the northern Dúnedain in alliance with a vassal Hill-men King of Rhudaur. King Argeleb I of Arthedain was killed leading the defense of his realm against Rhudaur and Angmar. In TA 1409 the Witch-king's army assaulted the fortress of Amon Sûl (Weathertop) and burned the tower, a conflict during which King Arveleg I of Arthedain was killed. The Witch-king then invaded and overran the Kingdom of Cardolan and annexed Rhudaur, expelling or killing the last Dúnedain.

<53>Now, the only resistance against his forces was the Kingdom of Arthedain. The Witch-king's armies invaded Arthedain and came close to destroying it, but King Araphor with the help of the Elves of Lindon and Rivendell managed to defend the capital, Fornost Erain, and drive back the invaders.

![](witchKing/6.jpg)

<53>An uneasy peace descended, but the deathless Lord of the Nazgûl could wage his war over hundreds of years. After the last surviving Dúnadan of Cardolan died in TA 1636, he sent evil spirits, the Barrow-wights, to the Barrow-downs in order to prevent the rebirth of that kingdom. Ultimately, in TA 1974 the Witch-king claimed victory in the north, when his forces captured Fornost Erain. With its fall, the final bastion of the old North Kingdom collapsed, and though Arvedui Last-King attempted to escape via the far north, he drowned in the Icebay of Forochel and the last vestiges of the kingdom of Arnor were extinguished.

<53>The Witch-king took his seat of power in newly-conquered Fornost, but the next year an army from Gondor under their general and crown-prince, Eärnur, landed at the Grey Havens. Too late to save their northern kin, they were nevertheless joined by the Elves of Lindon and the remnants of the northern Dúnedain, and together they marched against the Witch-king.

## **The Battle of Fornost**

![](witchKing/7.jpg)

#### Minas Morgul, the final seat of the Witch-king

<53>Eärnur's host did not meet the Witch-king at Fornost Erain itself, but on the plains to the west of it towards Lake Evendim, near the site of the ancient capital of the High Kings of Arnor, Annúminas. The struggle was however known afterwards as the Battle of Fornost. Crucially, Gondor's cavalry split off before the arrival of Angmar's forces and rode north into the Hills of Evendim to wait in ambush; when the main bulk of Eärnur's force met the enemy in battle, the cavalry emerged from the hills to strike the rear of the army of Angmar. This pincer movement served as the turning point of the battle, giving the Dúnedain a decisive advantage.

<53>Realizing his troops had been almost completely crushed, the Witch-king attempted to flee back to the east - but an Elven army from Rivendell led by Glorfindel emerged from the south and blocked his escape, destroying what was left of his crumbling army. As a last resort, the Witch-king revealed himself in person and challenged Eärnur. Eärnur made to assail him, but his horse was terrified by the Witch-king and reared. The Witch-king's laughter turned to flight when Glorfindel rode up to challenge him. When Eärnur attempted to follow him, Glorfindel stopped him with a warning that would become prophetic in the future:

> *"Do not pursue him! He will not return to these lands. Far off yet is his doom, and not by the hand of man will he fall."*

<53>The Witch-king, beaten, but having successfully brought down the kingdoms of the former Arnor, fled the north. Angmar was now leaderless, and soon collapsed.

## **Return to Mordor and the rise of Minas Morgul**

![](witchKing/8.jpg)

#### The Witch-king at Barad-dûr

<53>Despite the rout at Fornost, the Witch-king had accomplished his mission of destroying the northern Dúnedain kingdoms in Eriador, and so now he returned to Mordor. Though Sauron was still in hiding at Dol Guldur (disguised as "the Necromancer"), the Nazgûl rallied back around their chief, re-entering the Black Land in TA 1980. Together the nine Ringwraiths worked to rebuild their master's power in Mordor, gathering hordes of Orcs around themselves. In the year TA 2000, they issued from Mordor and attacked Gondor - which had been weakened by the Great Plague and the Wainriders invasion - and laid siege to Minas Ithil. The city fell two years later and the Nazgûl claimed the place for their own. It became known as Minas Morgul, the "Tower of Sorcery", and remained a place of great evil for centuries thereafter.

<53>In TA 2043 King Eärnil II of Gondor passed away and his son Eärnur, the Witch-king's old enemy, inherited the throne. Upon his coronation, the Lord of the Nazgûl challenged the new king to single combat, but Eärnur refused. However, seven years later in TA 2050 he challenged Eärnur again, and Eärnur accepted. Gondor's king rode out of Minas Tirith to meet his enemy in combat in Minas Morgul. He entered and was never seen again. Thus ended the reign of the Kings of Gondor, and began the rule of the Stewards of Gondor. In TA 2063 the Watchful Peace began, as Sauron fled Dol Guldur to avoid Gandalf's investigations and hid in the East, and the Nazgûl remained quiet in Minas Morgul for the next 400 years.

![](witchKing/9.jpg)

#### The Lord of the Nazgûl during the Battle of the Pelennor Fields

<53>The Watchful Peace ended in TA 2460 when Sauron, still identified only as the Necromancer, returned with increased strength to Dol Guldur. In TA 2475, the Lord of the Nazgûl sent hordes of newly-bred Black Uruks against Gondor's former capital Osgiliath, long decimated by war and plague, and seized its eastern half. The city was finally ruined, and the great stone bridge connecting the east and west banks of the River Anduin was destroyed.

<53>In TA 2941, after Gandalf finally confirmed that it truly was Sauron in disguise, the White Council belatedly moved to expel the Dark Lord from Dol Guldur. Sauron however simply retreated to his old stronghold of Mordor, declared himself openly once more in TA 2951, and began the rebuilding of Barad-dûr. He began preparations to find his One Ring, and sent three Ringwraiths to reoccupy Dol Guldur. In TA 3018, with the capture and torture of Gollum, the Dark Lord learned that his Ring was in the possession of a creature called 'Baggins' in a land called the 'Shire'. Unfortunately, Gollum had no idea where that was, leading to a fruitless search of almost a year. In June of TA 3018, the Witch-king and six other Wraiths attacked Osgiliath, then rode north to the Vales of Anduin. Finding the Hobbit dwellings there long-deserted and with Sauron's wrath growing, the chief Nazgûl led all Nine to Isengard, where Saruman refused to tell them the location of the land of the Halflings. However, the Lord of the Nazgûl chanced upon 'Wormtongue' Gríma in Rohan, who in fear for his life told where the Shire was.

## **The War of the Ring**

![](witchKing/11.jpg)

#### The Witch-king confronts Gandalf and Pippin in Minas Tirith

<53>The Ringwraiths rode swiftly north into Eriador, continuing to search for the "Baggins" that Gollum had told of until tracking the name to Crickhollow in Buckland. They raided Buckland but failed to find the Ring. During this time the Lord of the Nazgûl camped at Andrath, coordinating the searching Ringwraiths and stirring up the Barrow-wights.

<53>He then led four others of his kind to Weathertop - the ruined watchtower that he had destroyed 1600 years prior, where they discovered Frodo, Aragorn, and the other hobbits. The Nazgûl attacked the group, and their lord stabbed Frodo with a Morgul-knife. Though they were driven off by Aragorn, Frodo's wound threatened to turn him into a wraith like the Nazgûl. Fortunately for the afflicted hobbit, Elrond Half-elven sent Glorfindel to guide Frodo to Rivendell where Elrond could heal his wound, and in the race there Glorfindel lured the Ringwraiths into the River Bruinen. Here Elrond released a great flood, with Gandalf giving the waves of the torrent the form of horses. This flood destroyed the physical forms of the Nine, killed their horses, and sent the formless Wraiths back to their master in Mordor, buying the Fellowship time to plan their next move.

<53>With their return to Mordor, Sauron bestowed the Nazgûl with great winged creatures as their new mounts. The Dark Lord utilized the lesser eight Nazgûl for reconnaissance work; their leader, however, returned to Minas Morgul and resumed the role of commander of Sauron's forces. He then launched the campaign against Gondor, issuing from the Morgul Vale with a great host. There, at the bridge over the Morgulduin, he paused, sensing the Ring nearby - until Frodo touched the Phial of Galadriel, turning his enemy's thought aside. It would be the closest the Lord of the Nazgûl ever came to retrieving his master's prize.

## Siege of Gondor

![](witchKing/12.jpg)

#### The Witch-king fights Radagast

<53>The final Battle of Osgiliath was fought on March 13, 3019 against the garrison led by Faramir. Faramir's forces could not hold back the Orc hordes and retreated, first to the Causeway Forts and then to Minas Tirith, assailed by flying Nazgûl, losing many men in the retreat. With Gondor's defeat at Osgiliath, nothing stood in the way of Sauron's ambitions to destroy Minas Tirith and the strongest of his enemies.

<53>On March 14, a massive force of Orcs, Haradrim, and Easterlings marched on the gates of Minas Tirith. Sauron had given the Witch-king newfound strength; he appeared upon a black horse, and his presence cast a shadow of total paralysis and fear upon defenders and enemy. Directing the assault, he wasted no effort on the impenetrable outer wall of the city, instead focusing on setting the city alight, sapping the strength of the defenders with multiple siege towers before the main assault came against the Great Gate. Crying incantations in "some forgotten tongue", he enhanced the power of the battering ram Grond and weakened the already damaged gate of the city. Grond broke the gate, and the Lord of the Nazgûl entered.

<53>The defenders inside fled, struck with terror by his dark presence. However, his entrance was halted by Gandalf the White, the only one capable of withstanding his power. There was a duel of words between them, and the Nazgûl-king threw back his hood revealing a crown floating in empty air, mocking Gandalf as an "old fool" and commanding him to die. Threateningly he drew his blade, which burst into flames of its own accord. Gandalf held his ground, ready to do battle - yet, before they could clash, the horns of Rohan blew, signaling the belated arrival of help for Minas Tirith. The Ringwraith then withdrew to order the defense of Mordor's forces against this new threat.

## Downfall

![](witchKing/10.jpg)

#### The Witch-king's final moments

<53>The Witch-king mounted his winged beast and rallied his troops against the furious cavalry charge of the Rohirrim. The army of Rohan was slowed; their initial momentum overthrew many Orc companies but was then blunted by fresh reserves including Mûmakil - allowing the hosts of Mordor to reorganize. The Witch-king took this opportunity to strike down King Théoden while his troops were in confusion. Flying on his beast's back he swooped down upon the king, and the surrounding Rohirrim's horses panicked as his beast attacked. Théoden's horse Snowmane grew frightened, was struck by a black dart, and fell upon his master, crushing him.

<53>As the Nazgûl hovered on his great beast above the dying Théoden, the King's niece Éowyn and the hobbit Meriadoc Brandybuck stood in his way.

> **Éowyn**: *"Begone, foul dwimmerlaik, lord of carrion! Leave the dead in peace!"*
> **Nazgûl**: *"Come not between the Nazgûl and his prey! Or he will not slay thee in thy turn. He will bear thee away to the houses of lamentation, beyond all darkness, where thy flesh shall be devoured, and thy shriveled mind be left naked to the Lidless Eye."*
> **Éowyn**: *"Do what you will; but I will hinder it, if I may."*
> **Nazgûl**: *"Hinder me? Thou fool. No living man may hinder me!"*
> **Éowyn**: *"But no living man am I! You look upon a woman. Éowyn I am, Éomund's daughter. You stand between me and my lord and kin. Begone, if you be not deathless! For living or dark undead, I will smite you, if you touch him."*

<53>Éowyn slew his winged beast with a single stroke of her sword, severing its neck. The Wraith-king rose and, letting out a scream of hatred, he shattered her shield and broke her left arm with a single blow of his mace. But as he towered over her, preparing to deliver the final blow, Merry plunged his sword - an enchanted Barrow-blade, one of the few weapons that could have penetrated his defenses - from behind into the sinew of his knee, breaking the spell that bound his undead flesh to his will. While the Lord of the Nazgûl was distracted, Éowyn drove her sword where the wraith's head would have been, slaying him.

<53>The prophecy made by Glorfindel at the Battle of Fornost centuries before was fulfilled, as Éowyn was a woman. The Witch-king's death turned the tide of the battle, and ultimately the outcome of the War.

## Aftermath

![](witchKing/13.jpg)

#### The Witch-king's burial in The Hobbit: The Desolation of Smaug

<53>Only a few days later, during the last council of war before the assault on the Black Gate, Gandalf predicted that the Witch-king's defeat was one of several factors that would undermine Sauron's confidence in his forces' superiority. The Dark Lord's growing awareness that he was facing the Heir of Isildur in Aragorn, the shocking loss at the Battle of the Pelennor Fields, and the fall of his single most powerful servant all made it more likely that he would throw his entire remaining strength at whatever force Gondor and Rohan challenged him with in an attempt to annihilate them once and for all - thereby giving Frodo and Sam their one, slim chance of passing unhindered through Mordor and bringing the One Ring to Mount Doom:

> **Gandalf**: *"[Sauron] studies the signs: the Sword that robbed him of his treasure re-made; the winds of fortune turning in our favour, and the defeat unlooked-for of his first assault; the fall of his great Captain. His doubt will be growing, even as we speak here. His Eye is now straining towards us, blind almost to all else that is moving. So we must keep it."*

<53>This advice proved to be correct, and no more than ten days later Sauron himself was finally defeated when the Ring was unmade in the Cracks of Doom. The remaining Ringwraiths perished in the cataclysmic eruption of the mountain.

# Powers and abilities

---

![](witchKing/14.jpg)

#### View of the Witch-king's sword and armor

<53>As a Nazgûl, the Witch-king had a wide arsenal of powers. He was surrounded by an aura of fear, enough to send trained soldiers and horses fleeing in panic. He was a masterful duelist and warrior, capable of slaying even powerful enemies, or butchering lesser men. A cunning commander and politician, he was the mastermind behind the splintering and fall of Arnor and the near total annihilation of the bloodline of the Kings of Arnor and had a hand in the demise of the Kings of Gondor. Moreover, he almost conquered Gondor in the War of the Ring and led many successful attacks against the West.

<53>Aside from the common Black Breath, any weapon that struck the Lord of the Nazgûl would be destroyed (a fate that Merry Brandybuck and Éowyn's swords shared), and its bearer would be poisoned. This was so severe that it was worse than a blow from his mace. Unlike the other Nazgûl, the Witch-king was not daunted by rivers and could perceive more clearly in daylight.

<53>The Witch-king - as his title implies - was also a feared sorcerer, having powers over the physical world, breaking Frodo's sword with a mere gesture of his hand and weakening the Gates of Minas Tirith, allowing Grond to break them. In tandem with the final strike of Grond, he conjured at that moment a massive blast of lightning to bring down the gate that could be seen for miles.

<53>He was stronger at night. It was claimed he could create or thaw ice at his whim. He could set his blade aflame, and could send out and control Barrow-wights. His power fluctuated greatly throughout his existence, as his strength was bound to that of Sauron himself; the more powerful Sauron became, the more powerful the Nazgûl became. Because of this, he was, in the estimation of the White Wizard and the other Nazgûl, possibly equal to Gandalf in magic and power during the Battle of the Pelennor Fields.

# Weapons

---

## **Broadsword**

![](witchKing/15.jpg)

#### The Witch-king's sword

<53>In movies and game adaptations, the Witch-king's primary weapon is his flaming broadsword. It is seen in the game *The Lord of the Rings: The Third Age*, when he is attacking Éowyn and Berethor's party, who joined with Éowyn against the mighty Lord of the Nazgûl.

<53>Though it is not officially named in *The Lord of the Rings* book, those who pre-ordered *The Lord of the Rings: Conquest* video game gained a replica of the Witch-king's sword. On the box art (and playing as the Witch-king in-game), EA provides a name for the weapon: The Sword of Terror.

## **Mace**

<53>The Witch-king's other primary weapon was a large, deadly mace that can shatter shields or kill enemies in a single swing. Coupled with his sword, the Witch-king was an unstoppable force, vulnerable only to the combined efforts of an extremely lucky shieldmaiden and a hobbit whose blades were very powerful against the Witch-king specifically (a fact unknown to them).

<53>In the movies, the Witch-king uses a flail instead of a mace, but with the same effectiveness.

<53>In *The Lord of the Rings: The Battle for Middle-earth II*, the Witch-king is featured using a sword and a flail; however in the expansion pack, *The Rise of the Witch-king*, his character in the Angmar faction uses a steel sceptre, appearing as a small trident, and no flail.

## **Morgul-blade**

![](witchKing/16.jpg)

#### Morgul-Blade

<53>The Witch-king's smallest - yet still potent - weapon is a Morgul-blade, a deadly sorcerer's device that could slowly turn victims into a lesser wraith slave to the Nine and to Sauron. He used it on Frodo, in an attempt to claim the Ring and punish its bearer by putting him in a state worse than death. Fortunately for the hobbit, the blade failed to complete its task, as Frodo was swiftly brought to Rivendell and healed there by the ministrations of Elrond.

## **Fellbeast**

![](witchKing/17.jpg)

#### The fellbeast as depicted in The Lord of the Rings: The Return of the King

<53>Besides weapons crafted from forges in Mordor, the Witch-king also used his winged 'fellbeast' as a method of attacking his enemies. This beast served two purposes: to take down enemy structures and catapults and to kill massive amounts of enemies at once. When the Witch-king (or indeed, any other Ringwraith) swooped down upon his enemies, they would flee in terror and attempt to save themselves (usually unsuccessfully). The beast was finally slain by Éowyn via decapitation.
